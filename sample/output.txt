Please find below the AI-generated requirements analysis for Online Food Delivery Platform. Review each section and let us know if you have any feedback.
⚙️  Functional Requirements
▸ Customers must be able to register, log in, and manage their profiles.
▸ Customers must be able to search for nearby restaurants, view menus, and filter by cuisine or rating.
▸ Customers must be able to add items to a cart, apply promo codes, and place orders with credit card or UPI payment.
▸ Customers must receive real-time order status updates and live delivery tracking on both web and mobile apps.
▸ Restaurant owners must have a dashboard to create, update, and deactivate menu items, set availability, and view order history.
▸ Delivery agents must have a mobile app to accept or reject delivery requests, view navigation routes, and update delivery status.
▸ The system must send push and email notifications for order confirmations, status changes, and delivery completion.
▸ Admins must be able to manage users, restaurants, delivery agents, and view platform analytics.
🛡️  Non-Functional Requirements
▸ The platform must support at least 10,000 concurrent users with graceful degradation under load.
▸ Page and app load times must not exceed 3 seconds on a 3G connection.
▸ System availability must be 99.9% monthly uptime, measured by automated monitoring.
▸ All payment transactions must be PCI DSS compliant and use end‑to‑end encryption.
▸ The architecture must be horizontally scalable across multiple cloud regions.
▸ Data must be backed up daily with a recovery point objective (RPO) of 1 hour.
▸ The mobile apps must be compatible with iOS 13+ and Android 8+ devices.
⚠️  Risks
▸ Integration failures or downtime with third‑party payment gateways could block order completion.
▸ Real‑time location tracking may suffer latency due to poor GPS signal or network coverage.
▸ High concurrent traffic could exceed provisioned resources, causing performance degradation.
▸ Changes in regulatory requirements for data privacy or food delivery could require costly re‑engineering.
▸ Dependence on external map APIs may incur unexpected cost spikes or usage limits.
▸ Restaurant onboarding delays could limit the variety of available menus at launch.
💡  Assumptions
▸ Customers, restaurants, and delivery agents will have reliable internet access on smartphones or computers.
▸ Credit card and UPI are the primary payment methods and will be supported by selected providers.
▸ Restaurants will maintain up‑to‑date menus and availability through the provided dashboard.
▸ Delivery agents will use smartphones with GPS capability and will consent to location sharing.
▸ The platform will be deployed on a cloud provider that offers auto‑scaling and managed databases.
▸ User data will be stored in compliance with GDPR or applicable local privacy laws.
❓  Questions to Client
▸ Which payment gateway(s) and UPI providers should be integrated, and are there any preferred contracts?
▸ What are the specific rules for promo codes (e.g., discount type, expiration, usage limits)?
▸ Do you have a preferred map service (Google Maps, Mapbox, etc.) and any licensing constraints?
▸ Will the platform need to support multiple languages or regional cuisines at launch?
▸ Are there existing POS or inventory systems that restaurants need to sync with?
▸ What are the expected delivery zones and any restrictions on delivery distance or time?
▸ Do you require a rating/review system for restaurants and delivery agents?
▸ What SLA levels are expected for payment processing and order fulfillment?
